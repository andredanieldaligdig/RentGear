Browse Vehicles

Customers start on the home page and filter the fleet by vehicle type, location, and daily rate range. Each listing is intended to help them quickly compare practical use cases, approximate cost, and basic specs before they decide to continue.

Make a Reservation

In the current homepage design, clicking Reserve Now prompts the user to log in before completing the next step of the reservation process. If you later want a full booking form, the project already includes Firebase, MySQL, and Vercel setup notes so you can add a backend-backed reservation flow cleanly.

Recommended reservation process

  1. Customer selects a vehicle and starts the booking flow.
  2. Customer signs in or creates an account.
  3. Booking details are collected and sent to your backend.
  4. Operations team confirms availability, documents, and final pricing.

Pricing Guide

Displayed website rates should be treated as indicative base daily rates unless your final booking process expressly states otherwise. Total cost may depend on deposit requirements, delivery area, late return, tolls, fuel replacement, excess charges, vehicle damage, or route-specific commercial factors.

Typical pricing components

  • Base daily rental rate
  • Refundable security deposit
  • Delivery or return coordination charges
  • Late return or extension fees
  • Damage, toll, parking, or cleaning adjustments where applicable

FAQs

How quickly are reservations confirmed?

That depends on availability, operating hours, document review, and whether the vehicle is suitable for the intended use.

Do customers need an account?

An account-based flow works well with Firebase email auth, especially if you later want booking history or profile management.

Can pricing change after inquiry?

Yes. Final pricing may depend on actual dates, route, vehicle condition, operational availability, and applicable fees under the policy set.
